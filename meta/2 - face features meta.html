Face-related featuresÂ¶

A naive model that codes for the presence vs. absence of faces fails to identify robust FFA activation. We hypothesis that this reflects adaptation effects: the FFA is primarily responsive to the initial presentation of an entirely new face, or to the presentation of a previously seen face after a long delay. We test this by fitting separate models that include regressors coding for (a) first-time presentation of a face, (b) cumulative time each individual face has been seen for, and (c) time elapsed since a given face last appeared on-screen.
